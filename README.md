# **Sql Retail Analysis**

## **Project Overview**
This project analyzes the Superstore dataset using SQL(PostgreSQL) and tableau to identify business performance, profitability trends, discount impacts, and improvement opportunities.

The analysis focuses on answering several business questions commonly encountered in retail analytics and business intelligence.

## **Dataset Information**
Dataset : Sample Superstore

Main columns used in this analysis:
* Sales
* Profit
* Discount
* Quantity
* Region
* Category
* Sub-category

## **Business Questions**
1. What are the total sales and total profit generated by the company ?
2. Which region generates the highest profit ?
3. Which sub-category generates the highest profit ?
4. Which sub-category generates the highest loss ?
5. Does discount affect profitability ?
6. Which business areas offer the greatest opportunity for profit improvement ?

## **Tools Used**
* PostgreSQL
* DBeaver
* SQL
* VS Code
* Tableau Public
* Git & GitHub

## **SQL Analysis**
### **1. Total Sales and Profit**
Calculated total company sales and profit to understand overall business performance.

### **2. Profit by Region**
Compared sales and profit across regions to identify the most profitable and least profitable regions.

### **3. Average Discount by Region**
Analyzed discount behavior in each region to investigate potential impacts on profitability.

### **4. Loss-Making Sub-Categories**
Identified sub-categories generating negative profit.

### **5. Average Discount by Sub-Category**
Compared discount levels across sub-categories to determine whether high discounts are associated with low profitability.

### **6. Tables Root Cause Analysis**
Investigated individual transactions in the Tables sub-category to identify potential causes of losses. 

## **Key Findings**
### **Company Performance**
- **Total Sales: 2,297,201.07**

- **Total Profit: 286,397.79**

Although total sales are relatively high, profit margins remain limited compared to overall revenue.
### **Regional Analysis**
- West generated the highest profit.
- East generated the second-highest profit.
- Central generated the lowest profit.

### **Discount Analysis**
- Central had the highest average discount (24.04%).
- West had the lowest average discount (10.93%).

This suggests that aggressive discounting may contribute to lower profitability.

### **Sub-Category Analysis**
Most profitable sub-categories:

- Copiers
- Phones
- Accessories

Largest loss-making sub-categories:

- Tables (-17,725.59)
- Bookcases (-3,472.56)
- Supplies (-1,188.99)

### **Tables Investigation**
The Tables sub-category showed:

- High average discount (26.1%)
- Negative overall profit
- Multiple high-sales transactions generating negative profit

This indicates that excessive discounting may significantly reduce profitability despite strong sales performance.

## **Business Recommendations**
1. **Review Discount Policies**
Evaluate discount strategies, particularly for the Tables sub-category, to prevent excessive reductions in profit margins.

2. **Focus on High-Profit Products**
Increase focus on high-performing sub-categories such as Copiers while reassessing pricing and promotional strategies for loss-making products.

3. **Improve Central Region Performance**
Analyze business practices in the West region and apply successful strategies to lower-performing regions, especially Central.

4. **Monitor Profitability Alongside Sales**
Avoid focusing solely on sales growth. Profitability metrics should be monitored alongside revenue to ensure sustainable business performance.
